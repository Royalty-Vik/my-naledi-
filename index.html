About Hazel

Naledi Magane, 23, hails from the heartlands of Groblersdal, Limpopo — a soul sculpted by sunlight, silence, and Scripture. Now in her final year of Analytical Chemistry at Tshwane University of Technology, she merges precision with poeticism in both science and soul.

Far beyond academics, Naledi is a woman who listens to her spirit. Her journal is her mirror; her music, a map of her moods. She lives deliberately — absorbing nature, crafting meaning, and walking in quiet elegance. Every word she writes, every gaze she offers, speaks depth.

“The winds of change do not shake the rooted tree.”

She’s drawn to hues of violet blue, deep navy, and pure white — colors that echo her character: composed yet vibrant, delicate yet unshakable. Orchids and peonies are not just flowers to her; they’re metaphors for resilience and silent strength.
